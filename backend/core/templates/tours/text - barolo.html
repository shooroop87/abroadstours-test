What You’ll Experience

🍫 Alba – Truffle Capital of the World

Discover Alba — the gastronomic heart of Piedmont, internationally renowned for its white truffles, artisanal chocolate, and premium hazelnuts.

This isn’t a place for tour buses or souvenir stalls. Alba is reserved, refined, and blissfully untouched by mass tourism — a destination that rewards those with a taste for authenticity and elegance.

Meander through quiet, cobblestone streets framed by medieval towers and noble townhouses. Browse curated food boutiques, truffle specialists, and family-run pasticcerias that have perfected their craft for generations.

Around each corner, you'll find stories carved in stone — from Roman foundations to Renaissance façades. It’s a setting of understated charm and historical richness, ideal for travelers who prefer culture over crowds and quality over quantity.

Sip a perfect espresso in a sunlit square and experience what few others do: a peaceful, elegant Italy that still belongs to the locals.

🍷 A Family-Run Winery with 6 Top Wines

Savor 6 exceptional wines — including the iconic Barolo — at a boutique, family-owned estate surrounded by the quiet beauty of the Langhe hills.

This is not a commercial stop. It’s an intimate, curated tasting experience hosted by the winemakers themselves — a family with deep roots in Barolo's terroir and traditions.

You’ll be welcomed as a guest, not a tourist. Each glass reveals the elegance, complexity, and passion that define Piedmont’s finest reds — guided by people who live and breathe this land.

Sit back, enjoy refined local pairings, and take in views that stretch across UNESCO-protected vineyards. No crowds, no shortcuts — just timeless Italian hospitality, delivered with precision and pride.

🏰 A UNESCO Castle & a Glass of Wine with a View

Step into Grinzane Cavour — a perfectly preserved 11th-century castle rising above the rolling vineyards of the Langhe.

This UNESCO World Heritage Site isn’t just a castle — it’s a celebration of Piedmont’s wine legacy. Inside, you’ll find vaulted stone halls, noble history, and the region’s oldest enoteca: a curated collection of Barolo and Barbaresco beneath centuries-old beams.

Then comes the moment to pause. A hand-picked glass of wine, a quiet terrace, and panoramic views stretching across some of Italy’s most prestigious vineyards.

It’s an experience designed for those who appreciate heritage and elegance.

🍽️ Taste Real Italian Food – The Way It’s Meant to Be

In Alba, cuisine isn’t a meal — it’s heritage. Every dish tells a story, and every ingredient is a celebration of local excellence.

This is the birthplace of the Slow Food movement, where white truffles are revered like diamonds and hazelnuts become culinary treasures. Forget the tourist menus and factory-made flavors — here, you'll enjoy seasonal, artisanal dishes crafted by chefs who treat cooking as an art.

Surrounded by vineyards and Michelin-starred kitchens, Alba offers a dining experience that’s refined, deeply rooted, and utterly unforgettable. It’s not just food — it’s a way of life.
